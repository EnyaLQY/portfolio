    title: "User Testing"
    text: "<p>The high-fidelity prototype was handed to the development team for the code implementation. Meanwhile, to identify the unnoticed problems and improve the prototype, I conducted the usability test with 5 participants (different nationalities, religions and professions) at Cornell. The evaluation criteria of the usability testing includes effectiveness, efficiency, error rate and objective experience.</p><p>Considering we have a broad variety of audience including the general public, XMP team, researchers, non-government organizations and government agencies, it is challenging to accept all the user feedback and fulfill everyone’s needs. Our goal is to give the user the dataset and relevant information without biasing and give them the chance to judge whether what we are doing is right or wrong. So we should keep the objectivity of building a neutral, informational academic website. Therefore, we should prioritize the readability of the website to make them more accessible. To improve this, we changed the font size and added videos for different experiences other than plain text reading.</p><p>To Increase efficiency of the website, we changed the navigation bar by getting rid of the button and adding indicators to highlight the page the audience are on. Besides, we could emphasize Cornell credential by adding founder acknowledgement and changing the logo. These are powerful signs to highlight the association with Cornell and strongly defend our project.</p><p>The changes would be applied to both the Figma prototype and the actual website.</p>"
